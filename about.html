Generally speaking, ray tracing is a "physically accurate" simulation of how light interacts with different surfaces and a camera. At the core of ray tracing are the rendering equation and all of the mathimatical equations that describe interactions like diffusion, reflection, and refraction.

While this demo may look quite incredible, it is NOT completely physically accurate because it does not account for more difficult lighting properties like diffraction or specular reflection. Nevertheless, it does support the following interactions, along with mixing and matching different "amounts" to describe many different surfaces:

  • Diffusion - light scattering on rought surfaces
  • Reflection - perfect light reflection
  • Refraction - light bending when it passes between different refraction indices

Another major limitation of this demo is that it can currently only render spheres and a limited amount of triangles. More professional and complete ray tracers are able to utilize more sphape definitions along with support for meshes with millions or billions of triangles. This is how 3D models work, and how movie CGI is usually rendered.
